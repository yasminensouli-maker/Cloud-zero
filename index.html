What AWS assigns to a partner — and what that means for you

When AWS invests in a partner, it assigns a team. Understanding who does what inside that team is the foundation of knowing how to show up effectively. Most partners treat every AWS contact the same way. That is the fastest way to get deprioritized.

AWS invests its field time and co-sell energy in partners who show up with consistency, with relevant pipeline, and with a clear point of view on the customer problem they solve. The partners who earn designated coverage, who get pulled into strategic account conversations, and who receive field sponsorship for funding programs — they are not the largest partners. They are the most prepared ones.

PDM

Partner Development Manager

Your primary AWS relationship. Owns your tier progression, funding access, co-sell motion, and field introductions. The relationship that unlocks everything else — and the one most partners underuse.

PSA

Partner Solutions Architect

The technical counterpart. Validates your solution design, supports Well-Architected reviews, and gives you credibility with AWS account SAs in co-sell situations. Engage them before you need them.

PES

Partner Enablement Specialist

Drives your training, certification path, and accreditation completions. The fastest path through the knowledge requirements for tier progression runs through this relationship — not the AWS website.

Designated

Designated coverage

At Advanced and Premier, AWS assigns named account managers and SAs to work specific opportunities with you. This coverage is earned through consistent pipeline quality and field engagement — not through tier alone.

What AWS will not tell you

The things that matter and are never documented.

These are not opinions. They are patterns observed from inside the AWS partner development function — across hundreds of partner conversations, co-sell cycles, and program applications.

Co-sell · ACE

Your first engagement with an AWS account team is your most important one

AWS account teams form a view of a partner in the first one or two interactions. If the first ACE submission is vague, the first meeting unprepared, or the first ask unclear — that impression compounds. Rebuilding credibility with a field team takes far more time than building it right the first time. Treat every early engagement like it is being evaluated, because it is.

AWS co-sell overview →
Resell · Channel

Resell is not loved unless there is a plan and demand behind it

AWS field teams find resell motions operationally complex — they add billing layers, margin structures, and customer relationships to manage. A resell play without a demand generation plan and a specific customer segment adds friction without a clear payoff. Resell works when the partner brings a differentiated go-to-market and the capacity to drive end-customer demand. Without that, it stalls.

Funding · Quota

Using funding the wrong way can hit an account team's quota

Certain funding mechanisms — particularly POC credits and some MAP structures — can count against an AWS account team's revenue targets if they are applied to workloads that were already committed. This is rarely explained upfront. The result is that account teams become cautious about sponsoring funding requests, even for legitimate opportunities, if they have not established trust that the partner understands the dynamics.

MAP · Migration

MAP now covers GenAI — most partners still think it only covers migrations

The 2026 MAP expansion covers GenAI and agentic features built as part of modernizing existing applications, data, or workloads — not only traditional lift-and-shift migrations. Partners who were told they were not MAP-eligible because they do not do infrastructure migrations should revisit that conversation. ACE Eligibility

ACE Eligibility is a one-way door to receiving AWS-sourced opportunities

Without ACE Eligibility, AWS account teams legally cannot send you leads — even when they want to. Reaching Eligibility requires 10 validated partner-originated opportunities in a rolling 12-month window plus enrollment in an AWS Differentiation Program. Once achieved, the co-sell motion reverses direction: AWS starts bringing you opportunities rather than only validating the ones you bring.

AWS Marketplace

Marketplace is a co-sell channel, not a listing page.

Customers with Enterprise Discount Programs and Private Pricing Agreements are actively looking for solutions that draw down their AWS commit. A Marketplace listing puts your solution inside that procurement motion without a cold call. The partners treating it as a distribution channel — not a checkbox — are the ones closing deals faster.

The 2025 Forrester TEI study found professional services sellers on Marketplace see 50% faster deal closure and measurable new business profit. AWS Marketplace now supports express private offers — automated custom pricing based on partner-defined terms — reducing the operational overhead that used to slow partner-led deals.

The Private Offer Promotion Program (MPOPP), launched August 2025, lets ISVs pass AWS Promotional Credits to end customers at the point of private offer acceptance, with funding available the next business day. It is a structured demand incentive built directly into the transaction — not a separate funding application.

Channel Partner Private Offers (CPPO) allow consulting partners to resell ISV solutions through Marketplace with their own pricing, terms, and margin — without the ISV needing a partner billing infrastructure. For SIs carrying ISV solutions in their portfolio, this is currently the cleanest revenue structure available inside the AWS ecosystem.

ISVs · SaaS sellers

Private Offer Promotion Program (MPOPP)

ISVs can offer AWS Promotional Credits to customers as an incentive at private offer acceptance. Self-service through the AWS Partner Funding Portal year-round, with next business day delivery. New sellers and established sellers with renewals both qualify. A demand lever built into the transaction itself.

SIs · Consulting partners

Channel Partner Private Offers (CPPO)

Consulting partners create and transact Marketplace private offers on behalf of ISVs at their own pricing and margin. The ISV sets wholesale price; the partner controls the customer relationship and markup. 3% listing fee standard, 3.5% for CPPO transactions. No separate billing infrastructure required.

Migration · ISV Workload Migration Program

Direct credit disbursement via Marketplace

The ISV Workload Migration Program (WMP) now disburses credits directly to end customers for eligible SaaS deployments through Marketplace. This gives partners more flexibility in structuring customer deals while simplifying how migration benefits reach the customer — without routing through a separate claims process.

GenAI · AI Specialization

Marketplace as the GenAI discovery layer

Partners completing the Agentic AI Specialization module can publish to the AI Agents and Tools solution page on Marketplace — the destination AWS is directing enterprise customers toward for next-generation AI solutions built on Bedrock AgentCore, Amazon Q, and Nova Act. This is distribution without cold outreach.
